## Format Requirements

- Minimum 4 pages and maximum 10 pages.
- References are excluded from the page limit.
- Use 2-column ACM/IEEE conference format.
- The paper should be written in an academic style.

## Marking Criteria

| Weight | Criterion | Requirements |
|---:|---|---|
| 10% | Relevance | The research questions and methods should be relevant to computer science. |
| 10% | Significance | The work should have scientific importance and likely impact. |
| 30% | Methodology | Methods should show originality or novelty, be sound, and be clearly described. |
| 30% | Theoretical / Empirical Evaluation | Evaluation should be correct and complete, include efficiency analysis such as time complexity, and compare against the state of the art. |
| 10% | Scholarship | The paper should demonstrate understanding of the state of the art, cite relevant work appropriately, and provide correct bibliographic information. |
| 10% | Presentation | The structure, sentence meaning, grammar, formatting, academic style, length, and visual elements should be clear and appropriate. |

## Score-Level Summary

The marking PDF provides criterion weights but does not define detailed score bands. A practical interpretation is:

| Score level | Expected standard |
|---|---|
| Low score | Research problem is weakly connected to computer science; methods are unclear or unsound; evaluation is missing or incorrect; references and formatting are poor. |
| Pass level | Research problem and methods are relevant; paper has a basic but understandable method and evaluation; formatting broadly follows the required conference style. |
| Merit level | Methodology is clear and mostly sound; evaluation covers important results and efficiency; related work is understood and cited properly; presentation is polished. |
| Distinction level | Work has clear novelty and significance; methodology is rigorous; evaluation is complete and compared with strong baselines or state-of-the-art work; writing and figures are publication-like. |

## High-Priority Checklist

- Make the research question explicit and clearly related to computer science.
- Explain why the problem matters and what impact the work may have.
- Describe the method clearly enough for a reader to reproduce or understand it.
- Evaluate correctness, completeness, efficiency, and comparison against existing approaches.
- Use high-quality figures, tables, or graphs only where they support the argument.
- Keep the paper within 4-10 pages excluding references.
- Check all citations and references for correctness and consistency.
